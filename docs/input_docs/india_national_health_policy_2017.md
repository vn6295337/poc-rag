# India's National Health Policy 2017 - Executive Summary

## Document Overview
**Official Title:** National Health Policy 2017  
**Source:** Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India  
**Effective Date:** Implementation from 2017 onwards  
**Scope:** Comprehensive health system strategy for India replacing 2002 policy

---

## Core Policy Objectives

The National Health Policy 2017 aims to:

1. **Attain highest possible level of health and well-being for all at all ages** through preventive and promotive health care
2. **Ensure universal access to good quality healthcare services** without financial hardship
3. **Increase access, improve quality, and lower costs** of healthcare delivery
4. **Achieve Universal Health Coverage (UHC)** progressively

### Primary Goal
> "Attainment of the highest possible level of health and well-being for all at all ages, through a preventive and promotive health care orientation in all developmental policies, and universal access to good quality health care services without anyone having to face financial hardship."

---

## Key Policy Principles (10 Principles)

1. **Professionalism, Integrity and Ethics** - Highest standards in healthcare delivery
2. **Equity** - Minimize disparities by gender, poverty, caste, disability, geography
3. **Affordability** - Prevent catastrophic health expenditure (>10% monthly consumption)
4. **Universality** - No exclusion based on social or economic status
5. **Patient-Centered Quality Care** - Gender-sensitive, safe, convenient services with dignity
6. **Accountability** - Financial transparency and elimination of corruption
7. **Inclusive Partnerships** - Multi-stakeholder approach across sectors
8. **Pluralism** - Integration of AYUSH and traditional medicine systems
9. **Decentralization** - Community participation in health planning
10. **Dynamism and Adaptiveness** - Evidence-based improvements and innovation

---

## Strategic Policy Thrusts

### 1. Ensuring Adequate Investment
- Raise public health expenditure from 1.15% to **2.5% of GDP by 2025**
- Link resource allocation to State development indicators
- Mobilize taxes on tobacco, alcohol, extractive industries, pollution
- Leverage Corporate Social Responsibility funds

### 2. Preventive and Promotive Health
**Seven Priority Action Areas:**
- Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (sanitation)
- Healthy diet and regular exercise
- Control tobacco, alcohol, substance abuse
- Road and rail safety (Yatri Suraksha)
- Gender violence prevention (Nirbhaya Nari)
- Workplace safety and reduced stress
- Air pollution reduction

### 3. Organization of Public Health Care Delivery
**Seven Key Policy Shifts:**
- Primary care: from selective to comprehensive assured care
- Secondary/Tertiary: from input-oriented to output-based purchasing
- Public hospitals: from user fees to free drugs and emergency services
- Infrastructure: from normative to targeted approach
- Urban health: from token to scaled interventions
- Programs: integration with health systems
- AYUSH: from standalone to mainstreamed services

## Major Health Programs and Targets

### Quantitative Goals (By 2025, where specified)

**Health Status Goals:**
- Increase life expectancy from 67.5 to 70 years
- Reduce maternal mortality ratio to 100 per 100,000 live births
- Reduce infant mortality to 28 per 1,000 live births
- Reduce under-five mortality to 23 per 1,000 live births
- Reduce total fertility rate to 2.1 at national level
- Reduce neo-natal mortality to 16; stillbirth rate to single digit

**Disease Control:**
- TB: achieve >85% cure rate, reach elimination by 2025
- HIV/AIDS: achieve 90-90-90 targets (90% aware, 90% on treatment, 90% suppressed)
- Eliminate Leprosy by 2018, Kala-Azar by 2017, Lymphatic Filariasis by 2017
- Reduce blindness prevalence to 0.25 per 1,000
- Reduce premature mortality from NCDs by 25%

**Health Services Coverage:**
- Increase public facility utilization by 50%
- Maintain antenatal care coverage >90%
- Ensure 90% skilled birth attendance
- Achieve >90% full immunization of newborns
- Meet family planning needs of >90% at national level
- Control disease status in 80% of hypertensive/diabetic individuals

### Specific Focus Areas

1. **RMNCH+A Services** (Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child, Adolescent Health)
2. **Malnutrition and Micronutrient Deficiencies** - Comprehensive supplementation and fortification strategies
3. **Non-Communicable Diseases** - Screening for hypertension, diabetes, cancer, COPD
4. **Communicable Diseases** - TB, HIV, Leprosy, Vector-borne diseases control
5. **Mental Health** - Increase specialist creation and community-based support
6. **Women's Health and Gender** - Gender equality and violence prevention

## Implementation Strategy

### Human Resources for Health
- Increase medical and paramedical education capacity
- Attract specialists to underserved areas through incentives
- Support ASHA workers and community health volunteers
- Develop Public Health Management Cadre

### Financing and Purchasing
- Allocate majority of resources to primary care
- Implement strategic purchasing of secondary/tertiary services
- Preference: public facilities > not-for-profit > commercial providers
- Align government health insurance schemes with public goals

### Regulatory Framework
- Reform professional education councils
- Enforce Clinical Establishments Act
- Strengthen drug, device, and food regulation
- Control pricing of essential drugs and diagnostics

### Technology and Digital Health
- Develop digital health ecosystem
- Promote vaccine manufacturing capacity
- Health Technology Assessment frameworks
- Integrated health information systems

---

## Key Commitments

- Free comprehensive primary healthcare for all
- Reduced out-of-pocket expenditure and catastrophic health costs
- Universal immunization and disease surveillance
- Mental health and palliative care expansion
- Gender equality integration across programs
- Special focus on tribal and vulnerable populations
- 24/7 emergency care in public hospitals
- Health education and community involvement

---

## Policy Innovation Points

1. **Comprehensive Primary Care** through Health and Wellness Centers
2. **Health Cards** linking families to primary care providers nationally
3. **Strategic Purchasing** from both public and private sectors
4. **AYUSH Mainstreaming** with research validation of traditional systems
5. **Digital Health** infrastructure for continuity of care
6. **Disaster Preparedness** with retained excess capacity in health systems
7. **School Health Programs** as primary care delivery sites

---

## Implementation Status

- Active implementation since 2017
- Aligned with Sustainable Development Goals
- Building on progress from previous NHP 2002
- Focus on achieving specified quantitative targets by 2020-2025
- Ongoing capacity building and resource mobilization
