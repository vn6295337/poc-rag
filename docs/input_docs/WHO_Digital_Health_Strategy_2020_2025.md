# WHO Global Strategy on Digital Health 2020-2025 - Executive Summary

## Document Overview
**Official Title:** Global Strategy on Digital Health 2020-2025  
**Source:** World Health Organization (WHO), endorsed by 73rd World Health Assembly (2020)  
**Timeframe:** 2020-2025 implementation period  
**Scope:** Global guidance for integrating digital technologies into health systems

---

## Core Policy Vision

> "Improve health for everyone, everywhere by accelerating the development and adoption of appropriate, accessible, affordable, scalable and sustainable person-centric digital health solutions to prevent, detect and respond to epidemics and pandemics, developing infrastructure and applications that enable countries to use health data to promote health and well-being."

---

## Strategic Objectives (4 Primary Goals)

### 1. Promote Global Collaboration and Advance Knowledge Transfer
- Align countries and stakeholders on global digital health opportunities and challenges
- Maximize impact of collaborations and partnerships
- Assess and promote innovative health technologies
- Establish governance and knowledge-sharing mechanisms
- Support disease surveillance centers for epidemic response

### 2. Advance Implementation of National Digital Health Strategies
- Support countries to develop and strengthen national digital health strategies
- Build integrated, interoperable digital health ecosystems
- Develop human and institutional capacity
- Promote sustainable financing models
- Enable change management in organizations

### 3. Strengthen Governance for Digital Health
- Create robust governance structures at global, regional, national levels
- Promote standards for safety, privacy, interoperability, ethics
- Establish regulatory frameworks for emerging technologies
- Improve measurement, monitoring, research on digital health
- Develop guidelines for digital health tools and implementations

### 4. Advocate People-Centered Health Systems Enabled by Digital Health
- Place people at center (patients, families, communities, health workers)
- Promote digital health literacy and capacity building
- Support gender equality and inclusive approaches
- Encourage transparent decision-making and public participation
- Ensure equitable access to digital health resources

---

## Guiding Principles (4 Core Principles)

1. **Institutionalization Requires Country Commitment** - Digital health must be integrated sustainably within national health systems while respecting sovereignty and culture

2. **Integrated Strategy Essential** - Digital health initiatives must be part of wider health system strategy with:
   - Strong governance structures
   - Leadership, financial, organizational, human, technological resources
   - Costed action plans enabling multi-stakeholder coordination

3. **Appropriate Use of Digital Technologies** - Promote digital public goods that are:
   - Adaptable to different contexts
   - Interoperable with WHO standards
   - Ethical, secure, equitable, and cost-effective
   - People-centered, evidence-based, and inclusive

4. **Address Impediments in Least-Developed Countries** - Urgent investment needed to overcome:
   - Infrastructure gaps
   - Insufficient resources
   - Limited education and connectivity
   - Regulatory challenges
   - Technology access barriers

---

## Digital Health Definitions and Scope

**Digital Health** encompasses:
- Electronic health (eHealth) - ICT use in health
- mHealth - mobile health technologies
- Internet of Things (IoT) - smart connected devices
- Artificial Intelligence and machine learning
- Big data analytics
- Advanced computing and robotics
- Telehealth and virtual care
- Digital therapeutics
- Blockchain and distributed systems

**Key Emphasis:** Health data as sensitive personal information requiring high security standards, privacy protections, and ethical governance

---

## Implementation Framework (4-Part Action Cycle)

### 1. COMMIT
- Encourage voluntary commitment by countries and stakeholders
- National health strategies aligned with digital health objectives
- Multi-stakeholder governance structures

### 2. CATALYSE
- Create enabling environment for digital health scaling
- Foster collaboration and partnerships
- Promote innovation and knowledge transfer

### 3. MEASURE
- Develop metrics and performance indicators
- Monitor progress and implementation maturity
- Evaluate impact on health outcomes

### 4. ENHANCE AND ITERATE
- Continuously refine strategies based on feedback
- Adapt to emerging technologies
- Share learning across countries

## Core Components of National Digital Health Strategy

1. **Leadership and Governance** - Clear institutional framework
2. **Investment and Operations** - Sustainable financing models
3. **Services and Applications** - Scaled-up digital health interventions
4. **Integration and Sustainability** - Interoperable systems
5. **Standards and Interoperability** - WHO norms and open standards
6. **Flexible Digital Infrastructure** - Adaptable technology platforms
7. **Health Workforce Capability** - Training and digital literacy
8. **Legislation, Ethics, Compliance** - Legal and regulatory framework
9. **People-Centered Approach** - User engagement and inclusion

---

## Key Provisions and Policy Actions

### For Member States:
- Develop or review national digital health strategies
- Establish governance structures and multi-stakeholder engagement
- Define digital health architecture blueprints
- Adopt legal and ethical frameworks protecting data privacy
- Identify sustainable financing mechanisms
- Implement monitoring and evaluation systems
- Support workforce training and capacity building
- Promote interoperability and open standards

### For WHO Secretariat:
- Provide normative guidance and technical support
- Develop standards and best practice guidelines
- Facilitate knowledge sharing networks
- Support capacity building initiatives
- Promote research and innovation
- Monitor global progress on digital health

### For Partners (Private Sector, Civil Society, Academic):
- Contribute expertise and resources
- Develop innovative digital health solutions
- Participate in standard-setting processes
- Support implementation in low-resource settings
- Share knowledge and best practices

## Specific Digital Health Technologies Promoted

- **Telehealth and remote care** - Expand access to specialists
- **Electronic health records (EHR)** - Continuity of care and data exchange
- **Health information systems** - Integrated data management
- **Artificial intelligence** - Diagnosis, treatment optimization, prediction
- **Mobile health (mHealth)** - Accessibility and convenience
- **Big data analytics** - Population health insights
- **Internet of Things** - Remote monitoring and wearables
- **Blockchain** - Data security and supply chain integrity

## Monitoring and Evaluation

**Key Indicators Track:**
- National digital health strategy adoption
- Health system digital maturity levels
- Interoperability and standards implementation
- Digital health investment levels
- Workforce digital literacy rates
- Public health surveillance system functionality
- Health data governance frameworks
- Biennial strategic reviews and progress reporting

## Critical Context

### Global Challenges Addressed:
- Healthcare system inefficiency and high costs
- Limited access to specialists and services
- Data fragmentation and poor continuity of care
- Pandemic preparedness and disease surveillance gaps
- Workforce capacity limitations
- Digital divide in developing countries
- Cybersecurity and data privacy risks

### WHO's Triple Billion Targets:
- 1 billion more people benefit from universal health coverage
- 1 billion more protected from health emergencies
- 1 billion more enjoy better health and well-being

- Strategy endorsed May 2020 (WHA73)
- Active implementation from 2020-2025

- WHO providing technical guidance and support
- Biennial reviews of progress planned
- Ongoing adaptation to emerging technologies
