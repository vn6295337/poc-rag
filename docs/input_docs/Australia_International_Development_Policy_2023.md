# Australia's International Development Policy 2023 - Executive Summary

## Document Overview
**Official Title:** Australia's International Development Policy  
**Source:** Australian Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT)  
**Published:** August 2023  
**Scope:** Long-term development strategy replacing 2014 policy; focuses on Indo-Pacific region

---

## Core Policy Objective

> "Advance an Indo-Pacific that is peaceful, stable, and prosperous. A region that is predictable â€” where differences are resolved by international law and norms, and where we can cooperate, trade, and thrive."

**Overarching Goal:** Sustainable development and poverty reduction underpins regional stability and serves Australia's national interests.

---

## Policy Foundation: Who We Are

### Australia's Strengths
1. **Economy** - Strong, resilient economic system with free trade principles
2. **Institutions** - Democratic system with vibrant civil society and rule of law
3. **Expertise** - World-leading expertise in diverse fields
4. **Connections** - Multicultural society (300+ ancestries) with deep regional ties
5. **First Nations Heritage** - Oldest continuing culture; historical traders and diplomats in region

### Core Values
- International law and norms
- Human rights and freedoms
- Democratic governance
- Independent media and institutions
- Environmental protection
- Gender equality and disability rights
- Climate action and net-zero commitment

---

## Strategic Context: Complex World Challenges

### Global Challenges
1. **Climate Change** - Greatest shared threat; requires urgent adaptation and resilience measures
2. **Debt Crisis** - 60% of poorest countries in debt distress; impacts development services
3. **Digital Divide** - 2.7 billion people unconnected; mostly women and girls in developing countries
4. **Displacement** - 100+ million people displaced by conflict, violence, persecution (2022 record)
5. **Poverty** - 600 million still living in extreme poverty by 2030 projection
6. **Gender Inequality** - 132 years to close global pay gap at current rates
7. **Economic Instability** - Higher inflation, interest rates, protectionism, supply chain disruptions

### Regional Focus: The Indo-Pacific
- **Priority Regions:** Pacific, Southeast Asia, South Asia
- **Strategic Significance:** Where Australia's interests most directly affected
- **Key Challenge:** 22 of 26 nearest neighbors are developing countries; some fragile
- **Opportunity:** Extraordinary development progress in recent decades

---

## Four-Pillar Approach to Development

### 1. BUILD EFFECTIVE, ACCOUNTABLE STATES
- Support institutional development and rule of law
- Strengthen governance and transparency
- Combat corruption (affects 1 in 6 businesses)
- Develop public financial management systems
- Improve service delivery capacity

### 2. ENHANCE STATE AND COMMUNITY RESILIENCE
- Build resilience to climate impacts and natural disasters
- Strengthen disaster preparedness and response
- Support economic diversification and productive employment
- Improve social safety nets and health systems
- Address water security (1.6 billion lacking safe water by 2030)

### 3. CONNECT WITH AUSTRALIA AND REGIONAL ARCHITECTURE
- Strengthen bilateral partnerships with respect
- Support ASEAN centrality and regional cooperation mechanisms
- Promote ASEAN Outlook on Indo-Pacific
- Implement Pacific Islands Forum 2050 Strategy
- Enhance connectivity and knowledge exchange

### 4. GENERATE COLLECTIVE ACTION ON GLOBAL CHALLENGES
- Address climate change (adaptation costs $300B annually by 2030)
- Combat disease and health emergencies
- Manage displacement and forced migration
- Strengthen multilateral engagement
- Support UN Sustainable Development Goals

---

## How Australia Will Work: Key Principles

### 1. Forge Partnerships of Respect
- Listen to partner priorities and perspectives
- Move from transactional to genuine relationships
- Develop country and regional plans jointly
- Respect partner sovereignty and local solutions
- Recognize local leadership and expertise

### 2. Draw on Australia's Strengths
- Leverage economic expertise and institutions
- Share institutional knowledge and experience
- Deploy technology and innovation capabilities
- Utilize First Nations perspectives and knowledge
- Engage Australian expertise and people

### 3. Deepen Quality, Transparency, Accountability
- Focus on where Australia adds most value
- Improve evaluation and impact measurement
- Pilot real-time reviews and impact assessments
- Publish annual Performance Reports
- Create online data portal for transparency

### 4. Support Locally Led Change
- Strengthen local leadership and institutions
- Empower local civil society and organizations
- Establish Civil Society Partnerships Fund
- Support local actors to drive solutions
- Build local capacity and capability

### 5. Maximize Collective Impact
- Coordinate with multilateral partners
- Align with regional development initiatives
- Support implementing partners effectively
- Share knowledge and learning globally
- Work across sectors and stakeholders

## Core Commitments

### Gender Equality
- New international gender equality strategy
- Target: 80% of investments address gender equality
- New investments >$3 million must include gender objectives
- Address gender-based violence, economic inequality, leadership gaps

### Disability Equity and Rights
- New disability equity and rights strategy
- 16% of world population (1.3 billion) experience significant disability
- 80% of people with disabilities live in developing countries
- Lead by experiences and expertise of people with disabilities
- Support disability-inclusive development

### Climate Change
- Respond to accelerating climate crisis
- Increase climate investments and climate-focused programs
- Target: At least 50% of new investments >$3 million have climate objectives (2024-25)
- Aiming for 80% by 2028-29
- Address climate risks alongside adaptation and resilience

### Local Leadership and Civil Society
- New Civil Society Partnerships Fund
- Direct funding to local organizations
- Support grassroots development initiatives
- Strengthen community participation
- Enable local ownership of development outcomes

### Innovative Development Financing
- Expand beyond traditional Official Development Assistance (ODA)
- Australian Development Investments (ADI) to catalyze private sector
- Blended finance mechanisms
- Concessional lending for development projects
- Mobilize diverse funding sources

### Humanitarian Strategy
- New comprehensive humanitarian strategy
- Help reduce humanitarian need
- Build resilience to crises
- Support crisis-affected populations
- Improve preparedness and response

## Performance and Delivery Framework

### Performance Indicators (Three-Tier System)
1. **Context Indicators** - Global and regional development trends
2. **Results Indicators** - Development outcomes in partner countries
3. **Organizational Indicators** - DFAT's effectiveness and capability

### Implementation Mechanisms
- **Development Partnership Plans** - Country-level plans with partners
- **Strategic Partnership Frameworks** - Guidance for multilateral engagement
- **Whole-of-Government Governance** - Integration of development with diplomacy, trade, security

### Accountability and Transparency
- Annual Performance of Australian Development Cooperation Report
- Online data portal with program and impact information
- Third-party evaluations and impact assessments
- Biennial perception surveys with partners
- Regular reviews and adaptive management

---

## Regional Strategies

### The Pacific
- Strong longstanding bonds forged in crisis
- Shared aspiration for climate resilience
- Blue Pacific concept central to strategy
- Address public debt crisis (expected to double 2019-2025)
- Support economic diversification and women's empowerment
- Implement 2050 Strategy for Blue Pacific Continent

### Southeast Asia
- Deep economic and community ties
- ASEAN centrality and Comprehensive Strategic Partnership
- Support steady poverty reduction (50% to 13%)
- Invest in economic productivity and clean energy
- Implement ASEAN Outlook on Indo-Pacific

### South Asia
- Growing engagement with dynamic region
- 50% under 24 years; 1 million entering workforce monthly until 2030
- Support education expansion and resilient cities
- Use productive/sustainable Indian Ocean resource management
- Address climate change and social inequality

### Beyond Indo-Pacific
- Important partnerships in Africa, Middle East, Latin America, Caribbean
- Address food security, fragility, displacement
- Multilateral engagement on global challenges
- Support international development commitments

## Key Investments and Programs

- Disaster Assistance Response Team (DART)
- Pacific Australia Labour Mobility (PALM) scheme
- INOVASI Program (innovation in service delivery)
- Basic Education Quality and Access in Laos (BEQUAL)
- Civil Society Partnerships Fund (new)
- Australian Development Investments (ADI) (new)
- Humanitarian assistance and crisis response
- Climate adaptation and resilience programs

## Implementation Timeline

- **Now:** Building relationships and refreshing country/regional plans
- **2023-2024:** Implement new strategies (gender, disability, humanitarian)
- **2024-2025:** Scale climate investments and Civil Society Fund
- **Ongoing:** Develop evaluation systems, capability building, reporting
- **Long-term:** Rebuild Australian development capability and presence

---

## Underlying Principles

1. **Respect for partner priorities** - Not Australia-led, partner-responsive
2. **Quality over quantity** - Focus on high-impact interventions
3. **Whole-of-country approach** - Government, business, civil society, academia
4. **Evidence-based** - Research, evaluation, learning inform decisions
5. **Transparent and accountable** - Clear about resource use and results
6. **Long-term commitment** - Sustained investment in relationships and impact
