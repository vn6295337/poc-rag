# EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) - Executive Summary

## Document Overview
**Official Title:** Regulation (EU) 2016/679 - General Data Protection Regulation  
**Source:** European Union, Official Journal L 119, 04.05.2016 (Corrected OJ L 127, 23.5.2018)  
**Effective Date:** May 25, 2018  
**Scope:** Comprehensive data protection framework for all EU/EEA member states and organizations processing personal data

---

## Core Policy Objective

> "Protect the fundamental rights and freedoms of natural persons, and in particular their right to the protection of personal data."

The GDPR establishes a unified legal framework across the European Union to:
- Harmonize data protection laws
- Empower individuals with rights over their personal data
- Regulate how organizations collect, process, and store personal information
- Impose accountability and transparency requirements on data controllers and processors

---

## Legal Framework Structure

The GDPR comprises **11 Chapters** with **99 Articles** and **173 Recitals**:

### Chapter 1: General Provisions
- Subject-matter and objectives
- Material scope (applies to all personal data processing)
- Territorial scope (applies globally if affecting EU residents)
- Definitions of key terms (personal data, controller, processor, etc.)

### Chapter 2: Principles Relating to Processing
- Lawfulness, fairness, transparency
- Purpose limitation
- Data minimization
- Accuracy of data
- Storage limitation
- Integrity and confidentiality
- Accountability

---

## Core Principles (Article 5)

Personal data must be processed in compliance with:

1. **Lawfulness, Fairness, Transparency** - Must be processed based on legitimate grounds
2. **Purpose Limitation** - Data collected only for specified, explicit, legitimate purposes
3. **Data Minimization** - Only collect data necessary for purpose
4. **Accuracy** - Data must be accurate and kept up-to-date
5. **Storage Limitation** - Keep data no longer than necessary
6. **Integrity and Confidentiality** - Ensure security and prevent unauthorized processing
7. **Accountability** - Controller responsible for demonstrating compliance

---

## Key Rights of Data Subjects (Chapter 3)

### Transparency and Information Rights
- **Right to be Informed** (Articles 13-14) - Clear information about data processing
- **Right of Access** (Article 15) - Access personal data held about you

### Rectification and Erasure
- **Right to Rectification** (Article 16) - Correct inaccurate data
- **Right to Erasure** (Article 17) - "Right to be Forgotten" — request deletion under certain conditions
- **Right to Restrict Processing** (Article 18) - Limit how data is used

### Data Portability and Objection
- **Right to Data Portability** (Article 20) - Obtain and transfer personal data in machine-readable format
- **Right to Object** (Article 21) - Object to certain types of processing

### Rights Related to Automated Decisions
- **Right Against Automated Decision-Making** (Article 22) - Protection from purely automated decisions with legal/significant effects

---

## Obligations of Controllers and Processors (Chapter 4)

### Controllers Must:
- Implement data protection by design and by default (Article 25)
- Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) for high-risk processing (Article 35)
- Maintain records of processing activities (Article 30)
- Implement appropriate security measures (Article 32)
- Notify supervisory authorities of data breaches (Article 33)
- Notify affected individuals of serious data breaches (Article 34)
- Appoint Data Protection Officer where required (Articles 37-39)

### Processors Must:
- Process data only on instructions from controller
- Implement same security standards as controllers
- Assist controller in meeting GDPR obligations
- Document processing through Data Processing Agreements

---

## Legal Bases for Processing (Article 6)

Processing is lawful only if:
1. Consent obtained (freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous)
2. Contract performance
3. Legal obligation compliance
4. Vital interests protection
5. Public task execution
6. Legitimate interests pursued (balancing test required)

---

## Special Categories of Data (Article 9)

**Sensitive Data** receive additional protection:
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Political opinions
- Religious or philosophical beliefs
- Trade union membership
- Genetic data
- Biometric data (for identification)
- Health data
- Sex life or sexual orientation data

**Processing Restrictions:** Generally prohibited except for specific permitted purposes (employment, legal claims, vital interests, non-profit organizations, publicly disclosed by data subject, etc.)

---

## Data Protection Framework

### Data Protection by Design and Default (Article 25)
- Implement protective measures into processing systems
- Build privacy into products and services from inception
- Default settings must be privacy-protective

### Data Protection Impact Assessment (Article 35)
- Required for high-risk processing activities
- Assess necessity, proportionality, and risk mitigation
- Consult supervisory authority for very high-risk processing

### Data Protection Officer (Articles 37-39)
- Required for: public authorities, large-scale monitoring, special category data processing
- Independent role
- Responsible for monitoring compliance and handling inquiries

---

## International Data Transfers (Chapter 5)

Personal data may be transferred to non-EU countries only if:

1. **Adequacy Decision** - EU recognizes country as having adequate protection level
2. **Appropriate Safeguards** - Standard contractual clauses, binding corporate rules, codes of conduct
3. **Specific Derogations** - Explicit data subject consent, legal obligation, vital interests, public task

**Adequacy Decisions:** Currently granted to ~20 countries/territories (limited list, frequently updated)

---

## Governance and Enforcement (Chapters 6-8)

### Supervisory Authorities
- Independent national authorities in each member state
- Powers to investigate, enforce, and impose penalties
- Cooperation and consistency mechanisms

### Data Protection Board
- European Data Protection Board (EDPB)
- Coordinates supervisory authorities
- Issues guidelines and opinions
- Dispute resolution between authorities

### Enforcement and Penalties
- **Administrative Fines:**
  - Category 1: Up to €10 million or 2% of global turnover
  - Category 2: Up to €20 million or 4% of global turnover
- **Right to Compensation** - Data subjects can claim damages
- **Right to Lodge Complaint** - With supervisory authorities
- **Right to Judicial Remedy** - Challenge decisions

---

## Rights and Remedies (Chapter 8)

- Right to lodge complaint with supervisory authority
- Right to effective judicial remedy against supervisory authority
- Right to effective remedy against controller or processor
- Right to compensation for damages caused by GDPR violation
- Data subject can be represented by organizations

---

## Key Implementation Requirements

### Consent Management
- Freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous
- Granular (separate for each purpose)
- Easy withdrawal
- Default: opt-in (not pre-ticked boxes)

### Breach Notification
- Notify authorities within **72 hours** (except low-risk breaches)
- Notify individuals **without undue delay** for high-risk breaches
- Document all breaches

### Privacy Policies
- Clear, transparent language
- Accessible information about all processing
- Contact information for controller and DPO
- Information about rights and legal basis

### Third-Party Contracts
- Data Processing Agreements with processors
- Terms addressing data security, assistance, deletion
- Sub-processor authorization requirements

---

## Critical Context

### Why GDPR?
- Responded to digital economy challenges
- Addressed fragmented national laws (28 different regimes)
- Protected fundamental rights in digital era
- Responded to major data breaches and privacy violations
- Enabled digital single market with common standards

### Business Impact
- Applies to ANY organization processing EU resident data
- Affects global operations (extraterritorial scope)
- Compliance required regardless of location
- Compliance is continuous obligation, not one-time effort

---

## Key Compliance Obligations Summary

- Lawful basis for all processing
- Privacy notices and transparency
- Individual rights mechanism (access, deletion, portability)
- Data Protection Impact Assessment (when needed)
- Data Protection Officer (when required)
- Breach notification procedures
- International transfer mechanisms
- Vendor/processor management
- Record-keeping and documentation
- Training and awareness
- Regular audits and assessments

---

## Penalties Overview

**Non-Compliance Consequences:**
- Fines up to €20 million or 4% global turnover
- Reputational damage
- Operational restrictions
- Lawsuits and compensation claims
- Legal liability for executives/board members
- Loss of customer trust
